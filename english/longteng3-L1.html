💙 L1: The Day I Broke the Rules

龍騰高職英文 — 互動教學

📝
課文分析
Paragraph 1-2
▶ A few years ago, I was employed by the McDonald's Corporation. On "Ronald Day," I would dress up as Ronald McDonald and visit the community hospital's sick children. Most of these young patients were suffering from terminal illnesses. Thus, I would try my best to ease their pain.
幾年前，我被麥當勞公司雇用。在「隆納日」，我會穿上隆納麥當勞的服裝，去訪問社區醫院生病的孩子。這些年輕患者中的大多數患有絕症。因此，我會盡力減輕他們的痛苦。
Paragraph 3-4
▶ And I found it tremendously rewarding to be able to bring some laughter to the kids. One of the rules of my job was never to visit the children alone when I was dressed as Ronald, for I might frighten them with my painted face and red wig. I wasn't permitted to have any physical contact with the children either, since doing so might pass on germs to them.
我發現能為孩子們帶去一些歡笑是極其有意義的。我工作的規則之一是穿著隆納時不能獨自訪問孩子，因為我可能會用我的油漆臉和紅色假髮嚇到他們。我也不被允許與孩子有任何身體接觸，因為這樣做可能會傳播細菌。
Paragraph 5-6
▶ Though I understood the reasoning behind these rules, I also felt that sometimes nothing could convey love, empathy, and warmth like a heartfelt embrace. Once, as I was leaving the hospital after finishing my work for the day, I heard a voice coming from a half-open door. "Ronald," the frail voice called, prompting me to push open the door and go in.
雖然我理解這些規則的原因，但我也感到有時沒有什麼能像一個真誠的擁抱那樣傳達愛、同理心和溫暖。有一次，當我完成當天工作後離開醫院時，我聽到一個來自半開門的聲音。「隆納，」虛弱的聲音呼喊，促使我推開門走進去。
Paragraph 7-9
▶ I found a small boy of about five lying in bed and looking extremely weak and pale. "His name is Billy," his parents told me, smiling. It was really heartbreaking to see such a young boy confined to a hospital bed. I knew I had to do anything I could to cheer little Billy up, so I stayed and did some magic tricks for him. A sparkle soon came to his eyes and I asked if I could do anything else for him.
我發現一個大約五歲的小男孩躺在床上，看起來極其虛弱蒼白。「他的名字是比利，」他的父母對我微笑著說。看到這樣年輕的男孩被困在醫院床上真的很令人心碎。我知道我必須盡我所能讓小比利振作起來，所以我留下來為他表演一些魔術。他的眼睛很快閃閃發光，我問他是否還有什麼我可以為他做的。
Paragraph 10-13
▶ Billy replied, "Ronald, can I have a hug?" Although having to turn him down pained me deeply, I had no choice. I told Billy that it was against the rules and then quickly added that we could do some coloring instead. A while later, it appeared that he was getting drowsy, so I rose to my feet to get ready to leave. That was when he stretched out his tiny arms to hug me. I was about to refuse again, but then I saw the look of hope in his eyes. Suddenly, I realized I no longer cared if I was caught breaking the rules.
比利回答，「隆納，我可以得到一個擁抱嗎？」雖然不得不拒絕他讓我感到很痛苦，但我別無選擇。我告訴比利這是違反規則的，然後迅速補充說我們可以改為著色。過了一會兒，他似乎變得昏昏欲睡，所以我站起身準備離開。就在那時，他伸出了他的小臂來擁抱我。我正要再次拒絕，但隨後我看到了他眼中的希望。突然，我意識到我不再在乎是否被抓住違反規則。
Paragraph 14-16
▶ Some things in life, after all, were more important. I picked him up and held him in my arms. After that, we talked and talked. He confided his worries to me. He was afraid that his little brother would have no one to play with. Also, he was scared that his dog would go hungry because he had hidden all its bones. It seemed that Billy knew he would never be going home. And yet, even then, he was only thinking of others.
畢竟，生活中有些事情更重要。我抱起他，抱在我懷裡。在那之後，我們談呀談。他向我傾訴了他的擔憂。他擔心他的弟弟將沒有人和他一起玩。他也害怕他的狗會挨餓，因為他藏了所有的骨頭。看起來比利知道他永遠不會回家。然而，即使在那時，他也只是在想著別人。
Paragraph 17-18
▶ When I finally stood up to leave, I gave Billy's parents my number and told them not to hesitate to call me if I could be of any assistance. Two days later, I received a call from Billy's mom, who told me her son had passed away. She wanted to thank me for making a difference in his life, for he'd said to her, "Mommy, I don't mind if I can't see Santa this year because I've hugged Ronald."
當我最後站起來要離開時，我給了比利的父母我的電話號碼，並告訴他們如果我可以幫助，不要猶豫就給我打電話。兩天後，我接到了比利媽媽的電話，她告訴我她的兒子已經去世了。她想感謝我對他的生活做出了改變，因為他對她說，「媽媽，我不介意今年看不到聖誕老人，因為我已經擁抱了隆納。」

練習題
1
What was the narrator's job?
(A) A hospital nurse
(B) A person who dressed as Ronald McDonald for children
(C) A children's magician
(D) A McDonald's restaurant manager
✓ 答案：B
課文說：「I was employed by the McDonald's Corporation...I would dress up as Ronald McDonald and visit the community hospital's sick children.」敘述者是被麥當勞雇用來穿著隆納麥當勞的服裝訪問醫院生病孩子的人。
2
What rules was the narrator required to follow?
(A) Never visit the hospital after 5 PM
(B) Always bring gifts for the children
(C) Never visit alone and no physical contact with children
(D) Only visit children who speak English
✓ 答案：C
課文明確說明：「One of the rules of my job was never to visit the children alone when I was dressed as Ronald...I wasn't permitted to have any physical contact with the children either.」
3
Why did the narrator finally decide to break the rules?
(A) Billy threatened to cry if he didn't get a hug
(B) The nurse told him it was okay to break the rules
(C) He saw the look of hope in Billy's eyes and realized some things were more important than rules
(D) Billy's parents asked him to give Billy a hug
✓ 答案：C
課文說明：「I was about to refuse again, but then I saw the look of hope in his eyes. Suddenly, I realized I no longer cared if I was caught breaking the rules. Some things in life, after all, were more important.」
4
What was Billy worried about?
(A) He wouldn't be able to see Ronald again
(B) His little brother would have no one to play with and his dog would go hungry
(C) His parents would be sad
(D) He wouldn't see Santa Claus
✓ 答案：B
課文說：「He was afraid that his little brother would have no one to play with. Also, he was scared that his dog would go hungry because he had hidden all its bones.」比利擔心他的弟弟沒人陪玩，他的狗會挨餓。
5
What did Billy tell his mother about the narrator?
(A) He wished he had gotten more magic tricks
(B) He didn't mind missing Santa because he hugged Ronald
(C) He wanted Ronald to visit every day
(D) He wanted to tell Ronald about his worries
✓ 答案：B
課文最後說明：「she wanted to thank me for making a difference in his life, for he'd said to her, 'Mommy, I don't mind if I can't see Santa this year because I've hugged Ronald.'」
