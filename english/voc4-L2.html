📖 第一段 — 引入下午茶習俗
▶You may have heard of the delightful custom of sipping hot tea in the late afternoon.
你或許曾聽說過在下午時分品飲熱茶這個令人愉快的習俗。
句法結構 YouS may have heard ofV（情態助動詞+現在完成式） the delightful customO of sipping hot tea in the late afternoonM（介系詞片語修飾 custom）
may have heard of：情態助動詞 may + 完成式，表「對過去的推測」，意思是「也許已經聽過」。
hear of = 聽說（知道有這件事）；hear from = 收到……的消息。
delightful adj. 令人愉快的；sip v. 小口地啜飲。
▶But do you know who started this tea-drinking tradition?
但你知道是誰開創了這個飲茶傳統嗎？
句法結構（疑問句 + 間接問句） do you knowV（主要疑問句） who started this tea-drinking traditionO（間接問句，語序為直述句）
間接問句：do you know + who + 直述語序（S+V），不用倒裝。
✓ do you know who started...　✗ do you know who did start...
▶And are you aware that the British have both "low" and "high" tea?
你是否知道英國人既有「低茶」也有「高茶」？
句法結構 youS areV awareC（形容詞） that the British have both "low" and "high" teaO（that子句）
be aware that... = 知道……、意識到……。both A and B = 兩者皆有（A 與 B 都）。
▶Afternoon tea can typically be described as sitting down for a period of time in the afternoon to enjoy tea and some snacks.
下午茶通常可以被描述為在下午坐下來享受一段時間的茶飲與點心。
句法結構（被動語態） Afternoon teaS can typically be described asV（情態助動詞+被動） sitting down...to enjoy tea and some snacksO（動名詞片語）
be described as + Ving：被描述為……。typically adv. 通常地、典型地。to enjoy 是不定詞表目的「為了享用」。
📖 第二段 — 低茶的起源（公爵夫人安娜）
▶Some people believe that the English duchess Anna Russell might have started this custom in the mid-1800s.
有些人認為英國公爵夫人安娜·羅素可能在十九世紀中葉開創了這個習俗。
句法結構（may/might have + p.p. 對過去推測） Some peopleS believeV that...Anna Russell might have started this custom in the mid-1800sO（that子句，推測過去）
might have started：對過去事實的推測（本課重要文法），意思是「可能已開始」，但不確定。
duchess n. 公爵夫人；mid-1800s = 十九世紀中葉（約1850年代）。
▶At that time, members of the upper class normally had a light lunch and late dinner.
在那個時代，上流社會的成員通常只吃簡單的午餐，晚餐也很晚才吃。
句法結構 At that timeM（時間副詞） members of the upper classS hadV a light lunch and late dinnerO
upper class 上流社會；light lunch 簡單/清淡的午餐（light 此處表「不豐盛的」）。
▶However, Lady Russell grew extremely hungry between meals and found it very difficult to wait so long until dinnertime.
然而，羅素夫人在兩餐之間會感到極度飢餓，覺得要等那麼久到晚餐時間是非常困難的事。
句法結構（雙謂語 + 假受詞 it） Lady RussellS grewV₁（連綴動詞） extremely hungryC₁ and foundV₂ it假受詞（代替後方不定詞） very difficultC₂ to wait so long until dinnertime真正受詞（不定詞）
found it + adj. + to V：虛受詞句型，it 代替後方不定詞。
例：I find it hard to concentrate. 我覺得很難集中注意力。
grow（連綴動詞）= become，後接形容詞。
▶Her solution to this daily struggle was to ask for some snacks and tea at 4 p.m.
她解決每日煎熬的方法，是在下午四點要求提供一些點心和茶。
句法結構 Her solution to this daily struggleS wasV to ask for some snacks and tea at 4 p.m.C（不定詞片語）
ask for = 要求、索取（本課重要片語）。
struggle n. 掙扎、煎熬；solution to + N = 某問題的解決之道。
▶She later started inviting friends over for these little tea parties, and because of this, her idea soon spread throughout British high society.
她後來開始邀請朋友來參加這些小型茶會，也因此，她的想法很快傳遍了英國上流社會。
句法結構（複合句） SheS₁ started invitingV₁ friends overO₁ / her ideaS₂ spreadV₂ throughout British high societyM
invite sb. over = 邀請某人來（家裡）。because of this = 因此（介系詞片語，後接名詞）。spread throughout = 傳遍……。
▶Hosts at these events served high-quality teas and delicious snacks on luxurious low tables.
這些活動的主人在豪華的矮桌上供應高品質的茶和美味點心。
句法結構 Hosts at these eventsS servedV high-quality teas and delicious snacksO on luxurious low tablesM（地點介系詞片語）
serve v. 供應、端上（食物飲料）；luxurious adj. 奢華的、豪華的。
▶This explains why we now call this custom "low tea." Despite the word "low," low tea clearly represents British high culture.
這就解釋了為何我們現在稱這個習俗為「低茶」。儘管有「低」這個字，低茶顯然代表了英國的上流文化。
句法結構 ThisS₁ explainsV₁ why we call this custom "low tea"O（間接問句） / Despite the word "low"M（讓步介系詞） low teaS₂ representsV₂ British high cultureO₂
despite + N/Ving = 儘管……（後接名詞或動名詞，不接子句）。
represent v. 代表；high culture 高雅文化、上流文化。
📖 第三段 — 高茶的起源（工人階級）
▶High tea, by comparison, functioned as one of the main meals during the day for the working class.
相比之下，高茶是工人階級一天中的主要正餐之一。
句法結構 High teaS by comparisonM（插入語） functioned asV（function as = 充當、作為……的功能） one of the main mealsC
by comparison = 相比之下（本課片語）；function as = 充當、起……的功能；working class 工人階級。
▶In the seventeenth and early eighteenth centuries, people mostly worked in agriculture and ate their biggest meal at noon.
在十七世紀及十八世紀初，人們大多從事農業，並在中午享用最豐盛的一餐。
句法結構 In the seventeenth and early eighteenth centuriesM（時間） peopleS mostly worked inV₁ agricultureO₁ and ateV₂ their biggest meal at noonO₂
agriculture n. 農業（本課核心字彙）；at noon 在中午。
▶This was followed by a light supper in the evening.
在那之後，傍晚時分再吃一頓簡單的晚餐。
句法結構（被動語態表順序） ThisS（代指正午大餐） was followed byV（被動，緊接著……） a light supper in the evening受動者
be followed by = 緊接著……（本課重要句型），用被動表示「先有A，接著是B」。
例：Dinner was followed by dessert. 晚餐之後接著是甜點。
▶During the Industrial Revolution, however, laborers were working longer hours in factories.
然而，在工業革命期間，工人在工廠工作的時間越來越長。
句法結構 During the Industrial RevolutionM（時間） laborersS were workingV（過去進行式） longer hours in factoriesM
Industrial Revolution 工業革命（18–19世紀）；laborer n. 勞工、工人（本課核心字彙）。
▶These long hours made it not so convenient to eat a big lunch.
這些漫長的工時使得在中午吃頓豐盛午餐變得不那麼方便。
句法結構（使役動詞 + 假受詞） These long hoursS madeV（使役動詞） it假受詞 not so convenientC to eat a big lunch真正受詞（不定詞）
make it + adj. + to V：使某事（做起來）……（虛受詞句型，與 find it + adj. + to V 平行）。
▶When they got off work, they were starving.
當他們下班時，早已飢腸轆轆。
句法結構 When they got off workM（時間副詞子句） theyS wereV starvingC（形容詞）
get off work = 下班；starving adj. 極度飢餓的（比 hungry 程度更深）。
▶Therefore, these workers had high tea immediately after work, usually between 5 and 7 p.m.
因此，這些工人下班後立即喝高茶，通常在下午五點至七點之間。
句法結構 ThereforeM（結果副詞） these workersS hadV high teaO immediately after work, usually between 5 and 7 p.m.M（時間）
immediately adv. 立刻、馬上；between A and B = 在A與B之間。
▶This was an energy-packed meal with food that was as filling as possible, such as meat, vegetables, fish, and pies.
這是一頓充滿能量的餐食，搭配盡可能飽腹的食物，例如肉類、蔬菜、魚和派。
句法結構（as...as possible） ThisS wasV an energy-packed mealC with food that was as filling as possibleM（關係子句含 as...as possible 句型）
as + adj. + as possible = 盡可能……（本課片語）；energy-packed 複合形容詞，充滿能量的；filling adj. 易飽的。
▶Strong black tea was served in ordinary teapots, and diners were seated on high-backed chairs at the dining table.
濃郁的紅茶盛裝在普通茶壺中端上，用餐者則坐在餐桌旁的高背椅上。
句法結構（被動語態） Strong black teaS₁ was servedV₁（被動） / dinersS₂ were seatedV₂（被動） on high-backed chairs at the dining tableM
serve（端上）與 seat（使就座）均用被動。high-backed 高背的（複合形容詞）——「高茶」名稱由此而來。
▶This inspired the term "high tea."
這便啟發了「高茶」這個名稱的誕生。
句法結構 ThisS（指前文整體描述） inspiredV the term "high tea"O
inspire v. 啟發、激發；term n. 術語、名稱。
📖 第四段 — 茶文化的現代意義
▶Research shows that, on average, each British person drinks 3.5 cups of tea a day.
研究顯示，平均而言，每位英國人每天喝三點五杯茶。
句法結構 ResearchS showsV that...each British person drinks 3.5 cups of tea a dayO（that子句） on averageM（插入語）
on average = 平均而言（本課片語）；a day = per day 每天（頻率副詞）。
▶This indicates that teatime has become a fundamental part of British culture.
這表明喝茶時間已成為英國文化不可或缺的一部分。
句法結構 ThisS indicatesV that teatime has become a fundamental part of British cultureO（that子句）
fundamental adj. 基本的、根本的（本課核心字彙）；indicate v. 指出、表明。has become 現在完成式表示持續至今的狀態。
▶Afternoon tea is now a way of life all across the country, and it is no longer restricted to the upper class.
下午茶現在是全國各地的一種生活方式，而且它不再僅限於上流社會。
句法結構（it is no longer + adj./p.p.） Afternoon teaS₁ isV₁ a way of life all across the countryC₁ / itS₂ is no longer restricted toV₂（被動 + no longer 否定） the upper classO₂
is no longer + p.p.：不再……（強調情況已改變）。
be restricted to = 被限制於；a way of life = 一種生活方式。
▶In fact, people enjoy it in just about every corner of the world.
事實上，世界上幾乎每一個角落的人們都喜愛下午茶。
句法結構 In factM（插入語） peopleS enjoyV itO（代指午後茶習俗） in just about every corner of the worldM（地方副詞）
just about = 幾乎（nearly）；every corner of the world = 世界的每個角落。
🗣️ 對話精講 — Sophie × Kristin × Waiter
🔵 Sophie（藍）
▶Thanks for inviting me today. It's so nice to hang out with you and enjoy a nice afternoon tea.
謝謝你今天邀請我。能和你出來玩、享受美好的下午茶真的很棒。
句法結構 It形式主詞 isV so niceC to hang out with you and enjoy a nice afternoon tea真正主詞（不定詞）
hang out with sb. = 和某人出來玩/消磨時間；Thanks for + V-ing = 謝謝你……。
🟢 Kristin（綠）
▶My pleasure. We haven't had a good chat in a long time.
不客氣。我們好久沒有好好聊天了。
My pleasure. = 不客氣、這是我的榮幸（比 You're welcome 更正式有禮）。haven't had... in a long time = 好久沒……了（現在完成式）。
🟠 Waiter（橘）
▶Good afternoon ladies. Are you ready to order?
午安，女士們。請問你們準備好點餐了嗎？
Are you ready to order? 餐廳服務常用句，詢問是否要點餐。
▶Well, our Classic English Afternoon Tea set is really popular. It comes with different kinds of desserts, cakes, and sandwiches.
嗯，我們的經典英式下午茶套餐非常受歡迎。它附有各種甜點、蛋糕和三明治。
come with = 附帶、搭配；popular adj. 受歡迎的。
▶Yes, you can choose from this page for herbal tea, fruit tea, or milk tea.
是的，您可以從這一頁選擇草本茶、水果茶或奶茶。
herbal tea 草本茶；choose from = 從……中選擇。
▶No problem. And what would you like to drink? / I'll be right back with your order.
沒問題。那您想喝什麼？ / 我馬上帶著您的餐點回來。
I'll be right back. 餐廳常用語，「我馬上回來」。What would you like to...? 禮貌詢問偏好的句型。
🟢 Kristin（綠）
▶Is this served with drinks? / Okay. I'll have the Classic set with a rose lemon tea, please.
這有附飲料嗎？ / 好的，我要經典套餐，搭配玫瑰檸檬茶，謝謝。
be served with = 附有、搭配（被動）；I'll have... = 我要點……（餐廳點餐常用）。
🔵 Sophie（藍）
▶Hi. What do you recommend? / I'll have the same set, please. / I'll have English black tea, please.
你好，你推薦什麼？ / 我也要一樣的套餐，謝謝。 / 我要英式紅茶，謝謝。
What do you recommend? = 你有什麼推薦？（詢問服務員建議的實用句）
I'll have the same. = 我要一樣的（點餐常用）。

文法句型 × 3
① may / might have + p.p. — 對過去的推測 ▼
主詞 + may/might + have + 過去分詞 (p.p.) → 表示說話者對「過去某事是否發生」的推測，不確定程度：might < may
Anna Russell might have started this custom in the mid-1800s.
安娜·羅素可能在十九世紀中葉開創了這個習俗。（說話者不確定）
You may have heard of the delightful custom of sipping hot tea.
你或許已經聽說過品飲熱茶這個令人愉快的習俗。
句型意義例句
may have + p.p.過去某事「可能」已發生（較肯定）She may have left already.
might have + p.p.過去某事「可能」已發生（更不確定）He might have missed the train.
must have + p.p.過去某事「一定」已發生（強推測）You must have been tired.
can't have + p.p.過去某事「不可能」已發生She can't have said that.
✗ She might started this custom.（must/may/might 後須接 have + p.p. 才表過去推測）
此句型為高職英文高頻考點。課文明確示範 might have started 與 may have heard of 兩個例句。
② This was followed by... — 被動語態表順序 ▼
A + was/were + followed by + B → 被動式，表示「A 之後緊接著是 B」（表示順序/時間先後）
This was followed by a light supper in the evening.
（中午大餐）之後，傍晚再吃一頓簡單的晚餐。
The opening ceremony was followed by a grand dinner.
開幕典禮之後，緊接著是一場盛大的晚宴。
主動對應句：A light supper followed (after) the big lunch.
（主動語態：簡單晚餐在大餐之後）
常見錯誤：A was followed by B — 注意 B 是「跟在 A 後面的事情」，順序不要搞反。
學習重點：be followed by 常用於說明事件順序，在說明文、歷史類文章非常常見。
③ it is no longer + adj./p.p. — 強調「已不再」的否定變化 ▼
主詞 + is/are + no longer + 形容詞 / 過去分詞 → 表示某狀態在過去存在，但現在已不再如此（強調改變）
It is no longer restricted to the upper class.
它不再僅限於上流社會。（現在已普及各階層）
Afternoon tea is no longer a luxury; everyone can enjoy it.
下午茶不再是奢侈品；每個人都可以享用。
類型結構例句
否定形容詞is no longer + adj.He is no longer young.
否定被動is no longer + p.p.Tea is no longer restricted to Britain.
否定動詞no longer + V（一般動詞前）She no longer drinks coffee.
注意位置：no longer 放在 be 動詞/助動詞「之後」，一般動詞「之前」。
✗ She drinks no longer coffee.　✓ She no longer drinks coffee.
no longer = not...anymore（語氣更正式）；強調過去有、現在無的轉變，是課文結語的重點句型。
