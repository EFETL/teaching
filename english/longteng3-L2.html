🟣 L2: The Marshmallow Challenge

龍騰高職英文 — 互動教學

📝
課文分析
Paragraph 1-2
▶ How can twenty sticks of dry spaghetti, a yard of tape, a yard of string, and one marshmallow help build a stronger team? An innovative team-building exercise known as the Marshmallow Challenge promises to do just that. In this challenge, with only the materials mentioned above, teams need to build the tallest free-standing structure capable of supporting the weight of one marshmallow at the top.
20 根乾意大利麵、1 碼膠帶、1 碼繩子和 1 個棉花糖怎樣能幫助建立一個更強大的團隊？一項名為「棉花糖挑戰」的創新團隊建設練習承諾就能做到。在這項挑戰中，僅使用上述材料，團隊需要建立最高的獨立結構，能夠在頂部支持一個棉花糖的重量。
Paragraph 3-4
▶ With only 18 minutes to complete the task, the teams must learn to collaborate quickly. The Marshmallow Challenge, promoted by Canadian technology expert Tom Wujec, has been conducted around the world with architects and engineers, CEOs, lawyers, business graduates, and even kindergarteners. Over the years the challenge has produced some surprising results.
團隊只有 18 分鐘的時間來完成任務，必須學會快速合作。由加拿大技術專家 Tom Wujec 推廣的「棉花糖挑戰」已在世界各地與建築師和工程師、首席執行官、律師、商業畢業生甚至幼兒園學生進行過。多年來，這項挑戰產生了一些令人驚訝的結果。
Paragraph 5-7
▶ In Wujec's study, the average height of the structures constructed was 20 inches. The group that built the highest structure was, as expected, architects and engineers. CEOs performed slightly better than average, coming in third. Lawyers, on the other hand, performed below average, ranking second to last. And despite working to create the best plan, business graduates performed the worst.
在 Wujec 的研究中，構造的結構的平均高度為 20 英寸。正如預期的那樣，建築師和工程師建造了最高的結構。首席執行官的表現略好於平均水平，排名第三。律師的表現低於平均水平，排名倒數第二。儘管商業畢業生努力創建最好的計劃，但他們的表現最差。
Paragraph 8
▶ Amazingly, kindergarteners outperformed most of the adult teams, achieving the second-best performance. The impressive performance of the kindergarten group, whose structures were taller than those of most adult teams, can be explained by several reasons related to their unique way of working.
令人驚訝的是，幼兒園學生的表現超過了大多數成人團隊，取得了第二好的表現。幼兒園組的令人印象深刻的表現可以用幾個與他們獨特的工作方式相關的原因來解釋。
Paragraph 9-10
▶ One reason is that kids usually get started with what has been given to them right away, whereas adults usually don't start putting the structure together until they have spent most of the time planning. This leaves no time to adjust the design if it cannot support the marshmallow. Another reason is that unlike adults, kindergarteners are quicker to change their methods after failed approaches.
一個原因是孩子們通常立即開始使用給定的材料，而成人通常在花費大部分時間計劃後才開始組裝結構。這沒有時間調整設計是否無法支持棉花糖。另一個原因是，與成人不同，幼兒園學生在失敗的方法後更快地改變他們的方法。
Paragraph 11-12
▶ They rely on trial and error—if one method doesn't work, they quickly move on to the next. This approach gives them immediate feedback about what design elements work to give their structure height and stability. Still another reason for their success is that children tend to collaborate freely and naturally. They don't waste time deciding who is in charge. Instead, they are more focused on working together to figure out how to build the tallest structure.
他們依靠試錯法——如果一個方法不起作用，他們就快速轉向下一個。這種方法為他們提供了關於哪些設計元素可以提高結構的高度和穩定性的即時反饋。他們成功的另一個原因是孩子們傾向於自由自然地合作。他們不浪費時間決定誰負責。相反，他們更專注於合作，以找出如何建造最高的結構。
Paragraph 13-14
▶ The Marshmallow Challenge teaches us that age and experience are not the only routes to success. In order to solve problems, we must roll up our sleeves and get down to work right away, explore and experiment with new ideas, and collaborate effectively. In this way, we can surely keep the marshmallow at the top.
「棉花糖挑戰」教導我們年齡和經驗不是成功的唯一途徑。為了解決問題，我們必須捲起袖子立即開始工作，探索和嘗試新想法，並有效地合作。這樣，我們肯定可以將棉花糖保持在頂部。

練習題
1
What is the main objective of the Marshmallow Challenge?
(A) To build a structure that can hold food
(B) To build the tallest free-standing structure supporting a marshmallow at the top
(C) To create a competition for engineers
(D) To teach people how to organize teams
✓ 答案：B
課文說明：「teams need to build the tallest free-standing structure capable of supporting the weight of one marshmallow at the top.」
2
Which group performed the best in the Marshmallow Challenge?
(A) Architects and engineers
(B) CEOs
(C) Business graduates
(D) Kindergarteners
✓ 答案：A
課文說明：「The group that built the highest structure was, as expected, architects and engineers.」
3
Why did kindergarteners perform better than most adults?
(A) They had more experience with building structures
(B) They started immediately, adjusted quickly, and collaborated freely without wasting time on planning or hierarchy
(C) They were given more materials than adults
(D) They spent more time planning before building
✓ 答案：B
課文分析了幼兒園學生成功的三個原因：1) 他們立即開始工作 2) 他們快速適應和改變方法 3) 他們自由合作而不浪費時間決定誰負責。
4
How did business graduates perform in the challenge?
(A) They ranked first
(B) They ranked third, better than average
(C) Despite their careful planning, they performed the worst
(D) They ranked second to last, above lawyers
✓ 答案：C
課文明確說明：「despite working to create the best plan, business graduates performed the worst.」儘管努力制定最好的計劃，商業畢業生的表現最差。
5
What main lesson does the Marshmallow Challenge teach us?
(A) Age and experience guarantee success
(B) Age and experience are not the only routes to success; immediate action, experimentation, and collaboration matter
(C) Careful planning is the most important factor for success
(D) Only engineers and architects can solve complex problems
✓ 答案：B
課文結尾說明：「The Marshmallow Challenge teaches us that age and experience are not the only routes to success. In order to solve problems, we must roll up our sleeves and get down to work right away, explore and experiment with new ideas, and collaborate effectively.」
