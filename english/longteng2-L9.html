▶Have you ever heard the term "paparazzi"?
你有沒有聽說過「paparazzi（狗仔隊）」這個詞？
🔍 句構分析 youS Have...heardV（現在完成式） the term "paparazzi"O everM（加強語氣）
現在完成式 have + ever + p.p. 常用於詢問「曾經有沒有過...的經驗」。term（n.）術語；詞彙。此句是全文的開場問句，引發讀者興趣。
▶Or how about "Photoshop" used as a verb?
或者「Photoshop」被當作動詞使用的情形呢？
🔍 句構分析 Or how aboutM（詢問語氣） "Photoshop" used as a verbO（名詞片語）
how about + N/V-ing = 「...怎麼樣？」表提議或詢問看法。used as a verb 是過去分詞片語，後位修飾 "Photoshop"，= which is used as a verb。
▶Both of these are examples of eponyms.
這兩者都是「專名詞（eponyms）」的例子。
🔍 句構分析 Both of theseS areV（連綴動詞） examples of eponymsC（主詞補語）
eponym（n.）專名詞：由某人名、地名或品牌名衍生出的普通詞彙，是本課核心主題詞。Both of + 複數名詞 後接複數動詞 are。
▶An eponym is a word which usually started out as the name of someone or something but took on a more general meaning later on.
專名詞是一個詞彙，通常起初是某人或某物的名稱，但後來衍生出更普遍的意思。
🔍 句構分析 An eponymS isV a word [which usually started out as...but took on...]C（含關係子句）
關係子句內：started out / took onV（並列） a more general meaningO later onM
take on（片語）= 承擔；呈現（新含義）。later on = 後來；接下來。general（adj.）普遍的；一般的。start out as = 以...起頭/開始。
▶An example of an eponym that is derived from a person's name is "paparazzi."
一個由人名衍生出來的專名詞例子是「paparazzi」。
🔍 句構分析 An example of an eponym [that is derived from a person's name]S（含關係子句） isV "paparazzi"C
be derived from = 衍生自；起源於（被動式）。derive（v.）主動：derive A from B（從B衍生出A）；被動：be derived from B（衍生自B）。注意主詞是長名詞片語，動詞仍用單數 is。
▶It is said that this word came from a character called Paparazzo in the Italian film La Dolce Vita.
據說這個詞來自義大利電影《甜蜜的生活》中一個名叫 Paparazzo 的角色。
🔍 句構分析 It虛主詞 is saidV（被動語態） that this word came from a character called Paparazzo...真主詞（that子句）
It is said that... = 據說...（被動語態句型，虛主詞 It）。同義：People say that... / S is said to V。called Paparazzo = 過去分詞片語後位修飾 a character，= who was called Paparazzo。
▶Set in Rome, the film is about the writer of a popular newspaper column featuring celebrity gossip.
以羅馬為背景，這部電影講述一位專寫名人八卦的熱門報紙專欄作家的故事。
🔍 句構分析 Set in RomeM（分詞構句，被動） the filmS is aboutV（介系詞片語） the writer of a popular newspaper column [featuring celebrity gossip]C
Set in Rome = 分詞構句（被動），= Because the film is set in Rome。feature（v.）以...為特色；以...為主角。gossip（n.）八卦；閒話。celebrity（n.）名人。
▶Paparazzo is a photographer who works with the writer to reveal the secret lives of celebrities.
帕帕拉佐是一位攝影師，他與那位作家合作，揭露名人的秘密生活。
🔍 句構分析 PaparazzoS isV a photographer [who works with the writer to reveal...]C（含關係子句）
reveal（v.）揭露；透露。work with sb. to V = 與某人合作去做某事。secret lives = 秘密生活；不為人知的私生活。
▶After the film was released in 1960, people began to use the word paparazzo and its plural form paparazzi to refer to all photographers who chase celebrities around.
這部電影於1960年發行後，人們開始用 paparazzo 這個詞及其複數形 paparazzi 來指稱所有追逐名人的攝影師。
🔍 句構分析 After the film was released in 1960M（副詞子句）
peopleS began to useV the word paparazzo and its plural form paparazziO to refer to all photographers [who chase celebrities around]M（目的不定詞）
release（v.）發行；釋放。refer to = 指稱；提及。plural form = 複數形式。注意：paparazzo 是義大利文單數，paparazzi 是複數（英文借詞保留義大利文變化）。
▶Product-based eponyms such as Photoshop, on the other hand, are brand names that are commonly used to describe other similar products, or in this case an action.
另一方面，以產品為基礎的專名詞，例如 Photoshop，是品牌名稱，通常被用來描述其他類似產品，或在本例中是一個動作。
🔍 句構分析 Product-based eponyms such as PhotoshopS on the other handM（插入語） areV brand names [that are commonly used to describe...]C
on the other hand = 另一方面（對比前文）。brand name = 品牌名稱。be used to + V原形 = 被用來做...（被動 + 目的不定詞，注意與「習慣於」的 be used to + V-ing 區別）。
▶Photoshop is an image-editing software program created by Adobe Inc.
Photoshop 是由 Adobe 公司所創建的圖像編輯軟體程式。
🔍 句構分析 PhotoshopS isV an image-editing software program [created by Adobe Inc.]C（含分詞後位修飾）
image-editing = 複合形容詞（用連字號連接）修飾 software program。created by = 過去分詞片語後位修飾，= which was created by Adobe Inc.。
▶It is used to create, modify, or digitally combine images and photographs.
它被用來建立、修改或數位合成影像和照片。
🔍 句構分析 ItS is used toV（被動 + 目的不定詞） create, modify, or digitally combine images and photographsO
modify（v.）修改；調整。combine（v.）結合；合成。digitally（adv.）數位地。三個動詞 create / modify / combine 用 or 並列，共享同一受詞 images and photographs。
▶Since it was first released in 1987, Photoshop has become the standard software used by photographers and designers around the world.
自從1987年首次發行以來，Photoshop 已成為全球攝影師和設計師所使用的標準軟體。
🔍 句構分析 Since it was first released in 1987M（副詞子句，since + 時間點）
PhotoshopS has becomeV（現在完成式） the standard software [used by photographers and designers around the world]C
since + 時間點 配合主要子句的現在完成式 has become，表示「自從...就一直...」。standard（adj.）標準的。used by = 過去分詞後位修飾 software。
▶It became so widely known that over time, people began to use "Photoshop" as a verb to mean "to edit a photo."
它變得如此廣為人知，以至於隨著時間推移，人們開始把「Photoshop」用作動詞，表示「修圖」的意思。
🔍 句構分析 ItS becameV so widely knownC that...people began to use "Photoshop" as a verb to mean...M（結果子句 so...that）
so + adj./adv. + that... = 如此...以至於...（結果子句句型，為本課重要文法）。widely known = 廣為人知。over time = 隨著時間推移。
▶For example, no matter what software program is actually used, you could say, "The picture has obviously been photoshopped."
例如，無論實際上使用的是哪種軟體程式，你都可以說：「這張照片顯然被修過了。」
🔍 句構分析 For exampleM no matter what software program is actually usedM（讓步副詞子句）
youS could sayV "The picture has obviously been photoshopped."O（直接引語）
no matter what... = 無論...（讓步副詞子句，= whatever）。has been photoshopped = 現在完成式被動，表示圖片已遭修改。品牌名 Photoshop 小寫化並加 -ed，成為普通動詞過去分詞。
▶In the beginning, Adobe was not pleased that people were using their product name to describe the work of other companies' products, but the eponym has become too popular for them to stop its widespread usage.
起初，Adobe 並不高興人們用他們的產品名稱來描述其他公司產品所做的工作，但這個專名詞已變得太受歡迎，讓他們無法阻止其廣泛使用。
🔍 句構分析 In the beginningM AdobeS was not pleasedV that people were using...O（that子句）
but the eponymS has becomeV too popular for them to stop its widespread usageC（too...for sb. to V）
in the beginning = 起初；一開始（片語）。too...for sb. to V = 太...以至於某人無法做...（否定含義，為本課重要文法）。widespread（adj.）廣泛的。usage（n.）用法；使用。
▶As you can see, eponyms play an important role in creating new words in our society.
如您所見，專名詞在我們社會中創造新詞方面扮演著重要的角色。
🔍 句構分析 As you can seeM（副詞子句，引導總結） eponymsS playV an important roleO in creating new words in our societyM（介系詞片語）
play a/an...role in + V-ing = 在...方面扮演...角色（固定片語）。in creating 中 creating 是動名詞，作介系詞 in 的受詞。as you can see = 如你所見，常用於引導總結句。
▶Language is constantly changing with the adoption of new words and alternative uses for existing words.
語言隨著新詞彙的採用以及現有詞彙的替代用法而持續不斷地改變著。
🔍 句構分析 LanguageS is constantly changingV（現在進行式，強調持續） with the adoption of new words and alternative uses for existing wordsM（伴隨狀態）
constantly（adv.）持續地；不斷地。alternative（adj.）替代的；另類的。existing（adj.）現有的；現存的。adoption（n.）採用；採納（= adopting）。
▶Who knows what new words we will think up next?
誰知道我們接下來會想出什麼新詞彙？
🔍 句構分析 WhoS knowsV what new words we will think up nextO（間接問句）
think up（片語動詞）= 想出；發明；創造出（= come up with）。who knows = 誰知道呢（修辭疑問句，表示無人確知）。間接問句語序為「疑問詞 + S + V」，不倒裝：what new words we will think up（✓），不是 what new words will we think up（✗）。

文法句型 × 3
句型1｜It is said that... 據說...（被動語態 + 虛主詞 it + that子句）▼
It is said that + S + V... = People say that + S + V... = S + is said to + V (原形) / have + p.p. It is believed / thought / reported / known that + S + V...

這個句型用虛主詞 It 代替真正的主詞（that子句），動詞用被動式 is said，表示「據說...」。也可以替換成 is believed（據信）、is thought（據認為）、is reported（據報導）、is known（眾所周知）。

It is said that this word came from a character called Paparazzo in the Italian film La Dolce Vita.
據說這個詞來自義大利電影《甜蜜的生活》中一個名叫帕帕拉佐的角色。
It is believed that regular exercise can significantly improve mental health.
據信，規律運動能顯著改善心理健康。
不要用 "It says that..." 來表示「據說」。"It says" 只適用於引用文章/文字內容，例如：It says in the article that...（那篇文章中說...）。兩者意思和用法完全不同。
It is said / believed / thought / reported / known that... 在學測閱讀和翻譯中頻繁出現。另一種寫法：The word is said to have come from a character...（不定詞用 to have + p.p. 表示比主要動詞更早發生的事）。
句型2｜be used to + V 與 used to + V 的三向辨析▼
① be used to + V原形 = 被用來做...（被動語態 + 目的不定詞） 例：It is used to create images.（它被用來建立影像） ② be used to + V-ing/N = 習慣於...（be + 介系詞 to） 例：She is used to working late.（她習慣晚工作） ③ used to + V原形 = 過去習慣...，現已不再（助動詞） 例：He used to live in Paris.（他以前住在巴黎）

課文出現的是第①種：be used to + V原形，表示「被用來做某事」，是被動語態加目的不定詞。注意三種用法的關鍵差異：

句型to 後面中文意思例子
be used to + V原形不定詞（目的）被用來做...（目的）Photoshop is used to edit photos.
be used to + V-ing/N介系詞 to習慣於...（現在狀態）I am used to waking up early.
used to + V原形不定詞（助動詞）過去習慣...（已改變）I used to wake up late.
It is used to create, modify, or digitally combine images and photographs.
它被用來建立、修改或數位合成影像和照片。（① be used to + V原形）
最易混淆：② be used to + V-ing（習慣）vs. ③ used to + V原形（過去習慣）。判斷方法：看有無 be 動詞。有 be → to 是介系詞，後接 V-ing；無 be（直接 used to）→ to 是不定詞，後接 V原形。
學測超高頻考點！常出現「be used to + V-ing / used to + V原形」的選擇題。記住：be used to 習慣(現在)，used to 過去習慣(現在不再)。I used to be afraid of dogs, but now I am used to living with three dogs.
句型3｜too...for sb. to V（太...以至於某人無法...）▼
S + be + too + adj./adv. + (for sb.) + to V原形 = S + be + so + adj./adv. + that + (sb.) + can't/couldn't + V 例：The problem is too difficult for beginners to solve. = The problem is so difficult that beginners can't solve it. （這個問題太難了，初學者無法解決。）

too...to V 含有否定意義，即「太...所以不能...」。若不定詞的邏輯主詞與句子主詞不同，用 for + 受格 引出不定詞的邏輯主詞。注意：若不定詞的受詞與句子主詞相同，要省略。

...the eponym has become too popular for them to stop its widespread usage.
這個專名詞已變得太受歡迎，讓他們無法阻止其廣泛使用。
The coffee was too hot for the child to drink.
這杯咖啡太燙了，小孩沒辦法喝。
too...to 本身已有否定意義，千萬不可再加 not：✗ too tired to not sleep → ✓ too tired to sleep（太累了睡不著）。進階用法：too...not to V = 太...不可能不...（雙重否定 = 肯定）：She's too smart not to notice.（她太聰明了，不可能沒注意到。）
學測翻譯和克漏字都愛考 too...to 和 so...that 的互換。公式：too + adj. + (for sb.) to V ↔ so + adj. + that (sb.) can't + V。另外 enough to V（夠...可以...）也是相關句型：old enough to drive = not too young to drive。
