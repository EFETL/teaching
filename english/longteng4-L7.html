💙 L7: Iceland's Road to Gender Equality

課文分析
Paragraph 1-4
▶ Iceland is a small island nation known for its breathtaking landscapes, boiling mud pools, and stunning views of the northern lights. But how many people know that this country, with fewer than 400,000 people, is also home to the world's most gender-balanced society? For the last decade, Iceland has ranked number one on the World Economic Forum's Global Gender Gap Index. The path to equality began on October 24, 1975, when 90% of the female population participated in the "Women's Day Off" to protest against wage inequality and other gender-related social injustices.
冰島是一個以壯觀景觀、沸騰泥漿和北極光景觀而聞名的小島國。但有多少人知道這個人口不足 40 萬的國家也是世界上性別最平衡的社會的故鄉？在過去十年中，冰島在世界經濟論壇的全球性別差距指數中排名第一。平等的道路始於 1975 年 10 月 24 日，當時 90% 的女性人口參加了「婦女放假日」，抗議薪資不平等和其他與性別相關的社會不公正。
Paragraph 5-8
▶ That day, instead of going to work, cooking meals, looking after children, or doing domestic chores, Icelandic women hoping for change took to the streets in protest. With the majority of women going on strike, the country fell into complete chaos. Banks and factories came to a standstill as many of their employees were women, flights were grounded because there was no one to provide service, and without actresses, theaters had to cancel performances. Additionally, mothers striking all day long simply left children in the care of their husbands.
那天，冰島婦女沒有去工作、做飯、照顧孩子或做家務，而是走上街頭抗議，期待改變。由於大多數婦女罷工，該國陷入了完全混亂。由於許多女員工，銀行和工廠陷入停滯，由於沒有人提供服務，飛行被取消，沒有女演員，劇院不得不取消演出。此外，整天罷工的母親只是將孩子交給丈夫照顧。
Paragraph 9-10
▶ Since the mainly female-run schools and nurseries were closed, fathers were thus forced to take their children with them to work. Some men bought candy and colored pencils in a desperate effort to have their children entertained, while others offered rewards to their older kids to have them take care of the younger ones. This protest paralyzed the entire nation. However, if it had not been for this protest, public opinion would not have changed and support for gender equality would not have grown.
由於主要由女性經營的學校和托兒所被關閉，父親們被迫帶著孩子去上班。一些男人購買糖果和彩色鉛筆，試圖讓孩子開心，而另一些男人則獎勵年長的孩子照顧年幼的孩子。這次抗議使整個國家陷入癱瘓。但是，如果不是因為這次抗議，公眾輿論就不會改變，對性別平等的支持也不會增長。
Paragraph 11-13
▶ One year later, Iceland established the Gender Equality Council and passed the Gender Equality Act, banning gender discrimination in the workplace and in schools. Then in 1980, an even bigger triumph was achieved when Iceland elected the world's first female president of a democratic country. However, despite all the progress the country had made, the fight for gender equality was not yet over.
一年後，冰島成立了性別平等委員會並通過了《性別平等法》，禁止工作場所和學校的性別歧視。然後在 1980 年，當冰島選舉了民主國家的世界上第一位女總統時，取得了更大的勝利。然而，儘管該國取得了所有進展，但性別平等的鬥爭還沒有結束。
Paragraph 14-17
▶ In 2005, Iceland's gap showed that women, on average, still being paid only 64.15% of what men earned. So, on the thirtieth anniversary of the Women's Day Off, women organized another strike. They left work at 2:08 p.m.—the exact time at which they started working for free compared with men. In 2016, women in Iceland again left work early, this time at 2:38 p.m. This was to remind people that the average woman still earned just 70.42% of the salary that males usually given to men.
2005 年，冰島的差距顯示婦女平均只獲得男性薪資的 64.15%。因此，在「婦女放假日」三十週年紀念日上，婦女組織了另一次罷工。他們在下午 2:08 離開工作——她們開始相比男性免費工作的確切時間。2016 年，冰島的婦女再次提早離開工作，這次是下午 2:38。這是提醒人們，平均而言，婦女仍然只獲得男性通常給男性的薪資的 70.42%。
Paragraph 18-20
▶ Finally, Iceland decided to get tough on inequality. In 2018, it became the first country to legally demand that men and women be paid equally. The revolutionary new law required that companies with at least twenty-five employees obtain a certificate proving that they were following national equal pay policies; otherwise, they would face heavy fines. Today, this Nordic wonderland has some of the world's strictest laws on workplace equality. Despite having achieved so much, the country continues to take bold steps to eliminate gender discrimination and become a fairer society where every person is treated equally.
最後，冰島決定對不平等進行嚴厲打擊。2018 年，它成為第一個在法律上要求男女薪資相等的國家。革命性的新法律要求至少有 25 名員工的公司獲得證書，證明他們遵循國家平等薪資政策；否則，他們將面臨巨額罰款。如今，這個北歐樂園擁有世界上一些最嚴格的工作場所平等法律。儘管已經取得了很多成就，但該國繼續採取大膽措施來消除性別歧視，成為一個更公平的社會。

練習題
1
What event marked the beginning of Iceland's path to gender equality?
(A) Electing the first female president
(B) The Women's Day Off strike on October 24, 1975
(C) Passing the Gender Equality Act
(D) Creating legal equal pay requirements
✓ 答案：B
課文明確說：「The path to equality began on October 24, 1975, when 90% of the female population participated in the 'Women's Day Off'...」
2
What effect did women's strike have on Iceland?
(A) It had little effect on the nation
(B) It paralyzed the nation, affecting banks, factories, flights, and theaters
(C) Only women were affected
(D) It lasted only one hour
✓ 答案：B
課文詳細描述：「With the majority of women going on strike, the country fell into complete chaos. Banks and factories came to a standstill...flights were grounded...theaters had to cancel performances.」
3
In 2018, what revolutionary law did Iceland pass?
(A) A law banning all women from working
(B) A law requiring equal pay for men and women in companies with 25+ employees
(C) A law preventing women from striking
(D) A law banning gender equality
✓ 答案：B
課文說：「In 2018, it became the first country to legally demand that men and women be paid equally. The revolutionary new law required that companies with at least twenty-five employees obtain a certificate proving that they were following national equal pay policies.」
4
Why did women leave work early in 2005 and 2016?
(A) They were sick
(B) To demonstrate the wage gap by leaving at the time they began working for free compared to men
(C) To celebrate a holiday
(D) The government forced them to leave
✓ 答案：B
課文解釋：「They left work at 2:08 p.m.—the exact time at which they started working for free compared with men.」這是在2005年時，2016年則改為2:38。
5
Which of the following is NOT mentioned as an achievement in Iceland's gender equality journey?
(A) Establishing the Gender Equality Council
(B) Electing the first female president
(C) Banning women from working in politics
(D) Passing equal pay legislation
✓ 答案：C
課文沒有提到禁止女性從政。相反，冰島選舉了世界上第一位民主國家的女總統。
