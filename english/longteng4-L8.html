🧡 L8: Overtourism in Barcelona

課文分析
Paragraph 1-4
▶ In this busy European city, large groups of tourists are everywhere. They overflow the sidewalks and streets, taking pictures at landmarks like the "Sagrada Familia" and crowding narrow passageways leading to markets. The city in question is Barcelona, Spain, which each year receives more than fifteen times as many visitors as its actual population! Barcelona is suffering from a phenomenon called "overtourism." This occurs when an attraction becomes overrun with tourists to such an extent that the local residents' quality of life declines dramatically.
在這個繁忙的歐洲城市中，大量遊客無處不在。他們擠滿人行道和街道，在「聖家族教堂」等地標處拍照，擁擠狹窄的市場通道。所討論的城市是西班牙巴塞隆納，每年接待的遊客人數是其實際人口的十五倍以上！巴塞隆納正遭受被稱為「過度旅遊」的現象。當一個景點被遊客淹沒到當地居民的生活品質急劇下降的程度時，就會發生這種情況。
Paragraph 5-9
▶ The 1992 Olympics put Barcelona on the tourist map, and soon afterward the number of tourists flocking to the city began to increase. In 2017, the number of annual visitors to Barcelona grew to almost five times as large as that in 1992. As expected, local hotels and businesses benefited from this expansion of tourism. However, the city's residents had to bear the cost. This invasion of visitors has resulted in various problems. Complaints about problems caused by thoughtless visitors are common.
1992 年的奧運會將巴塞隆納放在了旅遊地圖上，不久之後，湧入該市的遊客數量開始增加。2017 年，巴塞隆納的年度遊客數量增長到 1992 年的近五倍。不出所料，當地酒店和企業從這次旅遊業的擴張中受益。然而，城市的居民必須承擔成本。這次遊客入侵導致了各種問題。對缺乏考慮的遊客引起的問題的抱怨很常見。
Paragraph 10-14
▶ "These people have no respect for others," said Emilio Shasta, a local who has lived in Barcelona his entire life. They play loud music late at night and throw garbage all over the place. Residents are also fed up with some visitors' inappropriate behavior, such as urinating and throwing up on streets outside nightclubs. Furthermore, some local tenants have even been forced to leave their apartments because landlords are partial to renting their places out through Airbnb. In this way, landlords can make more money by charging tourists higher nightly rents.
「這些人不尊重他人，」終身居住在巴塞隆納的當地人 Emilio Shasta 說。他們在深夜播放大聲音樂，並在各處扔垃圾。居民們還對某些遊客的不當行為感到厭煩，例如在夜店外的街道上小便和嘔吐。此外，一些當地房客甚至被迫離開其公寓，因為房東偏向於通過 Airbnb 出租其地方。這樣，房東可以通過向遊客收取更高的夜間租金來賺取更多的錢。
Paragraph 15-19
▶ As stated by Lucia Trebocini, the head of a Barcelona neighborhood association, "Many residents of this area are so sick and tired of tourists that they have decided to leave." Even I myself am also considering that option. The good news is that city officials are keenly aware of all these complaints. They believe that only by introducing practical measures can the impact of overtourism be reduced. Such measures include putting limits on the number of people who can visit certain hotspots at a time, and banning large tour groups in particularly popular areas.
巴塞隆納鄰里協會主席 Lucia Trebocini 說，「這個地區的許多居民對遊客感到如此厭煩，以至於他們決定離開。」連我自己也在考慮那個選項。好消息是城市官員充分意識到所有這些抱怨。他們相信只有通過引入實際措施才能減少過度旅遊的影響。這些措施包括限制一次可以訪問某些熱點的人數，以及禁止大型旅遊團進入特別受歡迎的地區。
Paragraph 20-23
▶ The city has also restricted the development of new hotels and ordered that websites like Airbnb take down their listings of guesthouses that are not licensed. Finally, the government has come up with a thorough plan that concentrates less on promotion and more on tourism management. It has been said that tourism is a double-edged sword. On the one hand, tourism provides local business owners with great economic benefits. On the other hand, it disturbs the lives of local residents and can cause numerous social problems.
該市還限制了新酒店的發展，並命令 Airbnb 等網站撤下未經許可的民宿列表。最後，政府制定了一項全面計劃，重點不在宣傳上，而在於旅遊管理上。有人說旅遊是一把雙刃劍。一方面，旅遊為當地商業所有人提供了巨大的經濟利益。另一方面，它擾亂了當地居民的生活，並可能引起眾多社會問題。
Paragraph 24-25
▶ Clearly, Barcelona is striving to deal with the negative aspects of tourism and is acting to strike a balance between the local community and international visitors. Only after the authorities have developed sustainable solutions will overtourism be manageable and stop causing problems in society.
顯然，巴塞隆納正在努力應對旅遊業的負面影響，並正在努力在當地社區和國際遊客之間取得平衡。只有在當局開發出可持續解決方案之後，過度旅遊才會得到控制，並停止在社會中造成問題。

練習題
1
What is "overtourism"?
(A) Too many hotels in a city
(B) When a destination has so many tourists that local residents' quality of life declines
(C) Tourists staying for too long
(D) Too expensive tourism packages
✓ 答案：B
課文定義：「This occurs when an attraction becomes overrun with tourists to such an extent that the local residents' quality of life declines dramatically.」
2
How many times more visitors does Barcelona receive compared to its population?
(A) Five times
(B) More than fifteen times
(C) Twenty times
(D) Ten times
✓ 答案：B
課文明確說：「each year receives more than fifteen times as many visitors as its actual population」每年接待的遊客人數是其實際人口的十五倍以上。
3
What problems do residents complain about?
(A) Only noise pollution
(B) Loud music at night, garbage, urinating and vomiting on streets, loss of housing
(C) Only loss of housing
(D) Only inappropriate behavior
✓ 答案：B
課文提到了多個問題：「play loud music late at night and throw garbage...urinating and throwing up on streets...forced to leave their apartments because landlords are partial to renting through Airbnb。」
4
What measures has Barcelona taken to address overtourism?
(A) Ban all tourists
(B) Limit visitor numbers, ban tour groups, restrict hotel development, and regulate Airbnb listings
(C) Increase tourism promotion
(D) Raise hotel prices
✓ 答案：B
課文提列了多項措施：「putting limits on the number of people who can visit certain hotspots...banning large tour groups...restricted the development of new hotels...ordered that websites like Airbnb take down their listings。」
5
What does "double-edged sword" mean in this context?
(A) Tourism is always bad
(B) Tourism has both positive economic benefits and negative social impacts
(C) Tourism is always good
(D) Tourism creates weapons
✓ 答案：B
課文解釋：「On the one hand, tourism provides local business owners with great economic benefits. On the other hand, it disturbs the lives of local residents and can cause numerous social problems.」雙刃劍指既有利也有弊。
